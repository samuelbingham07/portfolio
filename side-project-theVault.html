TheVault.

TheVault. is a digital phonebook designed to protect elderly users from impersonation scams, built for Sparkathon 2025, a designathon hosted by Pomona Ventures with 30 competing teams, by a 5-person team. It gives elders a familiar, personal reference they've built themselves so they can verify someone's identity before handing over money or information.

The Problem

Our user research through surveys and interviews surfaced a pretty consistent pattern. Elderly people get targeted by impersonation scams where someone poses as a family member or friend in distress, and they have no easy way to verify who they're actually talking to. Calling back or texting can both be spoofed, and the pressure of the moment makes it hard to think clearly. TheVault. gives them something to check first.

How It Works

The app is a personal phonebook the user populates themselves with photos, contact details, and notes about their real loved ones. When a suspicious call or message comes in, they can open TheVault., look up that person, and check details before responding. Large text, minimal steps, and a UI that feels familiar rather than overwhelming.

My Role

I worked across programming the demo, user testing, and visual design. The team ran a full Human-Centered Design process through research, ideation, prototyping, and multiple rounds of testing. We didn't place, but it was a great first designathon and I'd do it again!
