Terms of Service

Solo project  |  Cooperative dungeon crawler  |  Players: 4  |  ~45 min

Terms of Service is a solo cooperative dungeon crawler I made for Advanced Game Design, designed as an art game about tech platform dark patterns. Four players sit down to what looks like a normal dungeon crawl, but after every 3rd room a System Update card is read aloud and the rules change mid-session. Coins get introduced without explanation, ads start appearing on every tile flip, and by Act 3 there's a premium tier that lets you pay 5 coins to skip them. By the time you win, you've agreed to everything.

It comes directly from my background in product management. I've read enough monetization roadmaps to recognize the pattern, and a game felt like the most honest way to communicate it. Making someone feel a dark pattern is way more effective than explaining it!

How It Works

Each act has 9 tiles laid face-down (empty rooms, enemies, mechanisms, traps, and a boss that can't be flipped until the other 8 are cleared. On your turn you play exactly 2 action cards from a hand of 4: Move to navigate, Fight to chip down enemies, Activate to trigger mechanisms, Peek to look at a tile before entering, Heal to restore health, Pass to give another player a free card. Health and coins carry over between acts and never reset.

The System Updates trigger at fixed points across all three acts and stack on top of each other without replacing anything previous. Coins get introduced in Act 1, gambling mechanics and the "Cost of Living Adjustment" (a price hike on existing coin costs) show up in Act 2, and ads interrupt every tile flip in Act 3. The agreement slips from each update stay on the table for the whole game. You win by making it through Act 3 and hearing v3.3 read aloud: "Everything is free."

Production

I designed three dungeon tilesets in Aseprite, all the System Update and action cards, the coin economy, and the physical rulebook in Adobe Illustrator, with everything printed and fabricated at the Harvey Mudd Makerspace and Scripps Fab Lab.
