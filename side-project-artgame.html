Solo project  |  Cooperative dungeon crawler  |  Players: 4  |  ~45 min

A cooperative analog dungeon crawler where the game mechanics enact what it feels like to live inside a software platform. Players sit down to what looks like a standard dungeon crawl — but every few rooms, a System Update card is read aloud and the rules change. Coins get introduced without warning. Ads interrupt play. A premium tier gets offered to make the ads stop. By the time the game ends, the players have agreed to everything.

Every time you download an app you tap "I Agree" on something you didn't read, and continued use is treated as consent. A game felt like the right way to make someone feel that rather than just explain it.

Production

I designed three dungeon tilesets in Aseprite, all System Update and action cards, the coin economy, and the physical rulebook in Adobe Illustrator. Everything was printed and fabricated at the Harvey Mudd Makerspace and Scripps Fab Lab.
