Haunting of the Whitcomb Manor

Team: Ari, Dante, Elisa, Sam  |  GM-less cooperative horror RPG  |  Players: 4

Haunting of the Whitcomb Manor is a GM-less cooperative horror RPG my team made for Advanced Game Design, built as an adaptation of Escape the Dark Castle. Four players are teenagers exploring an abandoned manor, working together without a game master to navigate chapter cards, fight enemies, and try to get everyone out alive and sane. It was a really fun build!

How It Works

The manor is structured across three acts of increasing difficulty, each ending in a boss encounter before the next opens. There's no turn order — the group decides together who flips the next chapter card, which matters because some consequences only fall on the person who turned it. Each card opens with italic story text read aloud, then gives instructions: make a choice, roll a die, fight an enemy, or use an item.

Each character is built around three stats (Brawns, Brains, Charm) plus a special ability, and has three custom dice that correspond to their sanity tier. Combat works by placing chapter dice beneath the enemy card and having all players simultaneously roll their character dice, then matching symbols to remove the enemy's dice. Some enemies have doublets or triplets that require multiple players to coordinate. If any enemy dice survive the round, the enemy hits back. Players can also choose to rest instead of fighting, sitting out that round to recover 1 Willpower.

Each character tracks two resources: Willpower (starts at 15, represents physical stamina) and Sanity (starts at 12, represents mental fortitude). You can carry up to two items, one in each hand, distributed as a group from a face-up draw. The goal is to defeat the boss of Act III with everyone still alive and sane. If anyone hits 0 Willpower or Sanity, the game ends immediately.

The Sanity System

Sanity has three tiers (12-9, 8-5, 4-1), and each tier gives you a stronger character die. As your character goes insane, their rolls get more powerful. When you hit the second tier you also permanently unlock your special ability. You can also voluntarily sacrifice 1 Sanity at any time to reroll — so the whole game you're trading mental health for a better outcome. It's a really satisfying risk/reward loop that made every encounter feel tense.

My Role

  • Narrative design: wrote the chapter card story text, the premise, and the rulebook voice
  • Game mechanics: designed the sanity tiering system, the collective turn-order structure, and the resting mechanic
  • Visual design and fabrication: rulebook layout in Adobe Illustrator, hand-illustrated components, and all physical production at the Harvey Mudd Makerspace and Scripps Fab Lab
